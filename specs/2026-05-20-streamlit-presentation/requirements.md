# Requirements: Streamlit Presentation for Academic Audience

## Scope
Develop a Streamlit web application that serves as an interactive presentation based on the `souerce.md` document. The analysis must focus heavily on the consequences of powerful LLMs (like Claude Mythos) within the current ethical hacking framework, specifically addressing the legal and regulatory aspects (such as the EU AI Act).

## Target Audience
- Academic audience (Researchers, Scholars, Legal and Cybersecurity Experts).
- The tone should be formal, well-structured, and analytical.

## Key Content Areas
1. **Introduction to Agentic Vulnerability Discovery**: Claude Mythos capabilities.
2. **Impact on Ethical Hacking**: How the baseline of zero-day discovery changes from human-driven to industrialized AI processes.
3. **Legal & Regulatory Aspects**: Dual-use technology risks, EU AI Act compliance, and the legal burden on organizations and cyber risk governance.

## Tech Stack Requirements
- Python & Streamlit.
- Use polished UI elements suitable for an academic presentation (e.g., clean typography, structured layout with sidebar navigation).
